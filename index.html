Ashtracker

◆

Performance Measures

Chesapeake Commons began deploying updates to Ashtracker in April 2015. At that time, additional tracking measures were added to better gage system use and performance. Detailed logging began in June 2015. The data presented here are taken from a six-month period ending in December 2015.

◆

Overall

◆

11,884 page views by 2,367 unique visitors since May 2014

◆

6-month summary

◆

Served valid requests to 390 unique IPs in 36 states

3,912 page views (excluding EIP and CC)

Approximately 50 unique sessions per month

◆

Page traffic

◆

20% of users visited the landing page

60% viewed top-level facility pages (all have been seen at least once)

3% viewed facility → contaminant pages

7% viewed well profile pages

1% viewed well → contaminant pages

Among users accessing the landing page, roughly 42% enter the site and then leave rather than continuing on to other pages. The overall bounce rate is approximately 38%.

Recommendation: Concentrate future development on high-traffic areas, specifically the landing page and facility profiles. Determine what information is critical to include in facility profiles and summarize data more clearly. Users would likely benefit from more organized, streamlined content. Additionally, note that users arriving via search engines primarily use facility names as query terms. Implementing Google's Sitelinks Search Box could offer a way for users to find facilities directly from the Google search platform.

◆

Traffic patterns

◆

75% of facility page views are from origins other than the landing page. The landing page isn't a high-volume funnel to facility pages.

About 21% of users viewed 10 or more pages, so there is an appetite for content.

In terms of page requests, there is 100% use for facilities but only 5% for wells.

9% of visitors return to Ashtracker more than once.

Recommendation: Move deep-lying information up the page hierarchy and increase the searchability of facility profiles. In addition, capture the attention of users by including more information on the landing page, specifically: recent facility updates; summary statistics; introductory text; curated analyses and key findings; and global keyword search. Another strategy is to create more access points and internal links throughout the site to encourage exploration.

In terms of retention, Ashtracker could offer subscriptions to a feed or notification list that receives updates about changes and reports as they're released by EIP. The system could also integrate with the existing Twitter account to trigger alerts when new data is brought online. Finally, the range of profiles could be expanded to include companies and/or geographies so that users not interested by specific details would still have useful content to consume.

◆

Data access

◆

9% of visitors used the reading explorer page

9 export requests from 8 users at 7 facilities

Recommendation: Take advantage of facility page use to increase data access. Users may balk at providing an email address, so an alternative could be to offer file downloads again or retain the current export method while removing the email input. Moving the data download button to a more prominent location and adding tags for tracking may also be beneficial.
